African American Women in the Civil Rights Movement: A bibliography

Chris Pescador
Pitzer College
Spring, 1996


Books, Bibliographies and Reference Guides

Basty, William H. & Klatman, Phyllis R., ed. (1978) The Black Family and the Black Woman - A Bibliography.
Pages 68-92. Information on Ella Baker, Septima Clark, and Fannie Lou Hamer.

Davis, Marianna W., ed. (1981) Contributions of Black Women to America. Vol. II.
Pages 126-168. Information on activists such as Daisy Bates, Fannie Lou Hamer, Septima Clark, etc. Photographs. Pages 170-176 Contains a bibliography.

D'Emilio, John (1979) The Civil Rights Struggle: Leaders in Profile.
Pages 31-32 Daisy Bates; Pages 66-67 Fannie Lou Hamer; Pages 81-82 Coretta Scott King
Brief summary of their roles in the civil rights movement.

Estell, Kenneth (1994) The African-American Almanac. Sixth Edition.
Pages 378-393 Information about Ella Baker, Daisy Bates, Angela Davis, Fannie Lou Hamer, Coretta Scott King, and Rosa Parks. Summary of each women's role in the civil rights movement (photographs).

Hardy, Gayle J. (1993) American Women Civil Rights Activists: Bibliographies of 68 Leaders, 1825-1992.
Pages 32-38 Daisy Bates
Pages 103-116 Angela Davis
Pages 176-179 Fannie Lou Hamer
Pages 223-232 Coretta Scott King
Pages 305-311 Rosa Parks

Hawkins, Walter L. (1992) African-American Biographies: Profiles of 558 Current Men and Women.
Page 329 Information on Rosa Parks (life story and her significance to the civil rights movement).
Pages 421-422 Contains a summary of the life of Alice Walker.

Hine, Darlene C., ed. (1993) Black Women in America: An Historical Encyclopedia.
Pages 229-239 Prominent women in the civil rights movement.
Included are: Angela Davis, Coretta Scott King, etc. Photos.
Pages 239-241 Contains a Bibliography.

Hornsby, Alton (1991) Chronology of African-American History.
Pages 158-159 Angela Davis
Pages 243-244 Coretta Scott King
Page 335 Rosa Parks
Pages 355-356 Septima Clark
Their roles in the civil rights movement.

Levy, Peter B., ed. (1992) Documentary History of the Modern Civil Rights Movement.
Pages 25-26 Septima Clark
Pages 70-74 Ella Baker
Pages 139-142 Fannie Lou Hamer
Their lives and their roles in the civil rights movement.

Lowry, Charles D., & Marsyolek, John F., ed. (1993) Encyclopedia of African-American Civil Rights: From Emancipation to the Present.
Pages 35-42, 103. Ella Baker, Septima Clark, Fannie Lou Hamer, etc. Photographs and bibliography.

Murray, Paul T. (1993) The Civil Rights Movement: References and Resources. Ella Baker and Fannie Lou Hamer.

Lerner, Gerda ed.(1972) Black Women in White America: A Documentary History.
Pages 345-352 Ella Baker
Pages 609-614 Fannie Lou Hamer.

Salem, Dorothy C., ed. (1993) African-American Women: A Biographical Dictionary.
A list of African-American women who were civil rights activists. Their lives and their significance to the movement.
Pages 25-29 Ella Baker
Pages 109-110 Septima Clark
Pages 142-145 Angela Davis
Pages 220-224 Fannie Lou Hamer
Pages 311-313 Coretta Scott King
Pages 381-383 Rosa Parks
Pages 528-530 Alice Walker

Smith, Jessie C., ed. (1992) Notable Black American Women.
Pages 40-43 Ella Baker
Pages 64-69 Daisy Bates
Pages 189-193 Septima Clark
Pages 250-253 Angela Davis
Pages 820-823 Rosa Parks

Journal Articles

Gender & Society, 1993 7(2): pages 162-182. B. Barnett. Invisible Southern Black Women Leaders in the Civil Rights Movement: The Triple Constraints of Gender, Race, and Class.

Sage: A Scholarly Journal on Black Women, 1988 5(2): pages 60-64 D. Bryan. Her-Story Unsilenced: Black Female Activists in the Civil Rights Movement
